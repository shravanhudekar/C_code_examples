It's common to feel nervous or get "blank" during an interview or other high-pressure situations. Here are some things you can do to overcome this:

Practice: Practice answering questions in front of a mirror or with a friend. The more you practice, the more comfortable you will feel.

Prepare: Make sure you understand the question being asked and have thought about your answer in advance.

Take a deep breath: If you feel yourself getting blank, take a deep breath and take your time to form your answer.

Be honest: If you don't know the answer to a question, it's better to admit it and try to understand the interviewer's perspective.

Try to relax: Remind yourself that it's okay to make mistakes and that the interviewer is not looking for perfection.

Speak confidently: Speak clearly and make eye contact with the interviewer, it helps to build confidence and convey your message effectively.

Use positive body language: Sit up straight, make eye contact, and use gestures that reflect confidence.

Stay calm: Don't let your nerves get the best of you, stay calm and remember that the interviewer is on your side.

Remember that it's normal to feel nervous, and that the more you practice and prepare, the more comfortable you will become with answering questions. With time and practice, you will be able to overcome this hurdle and make a great impression during your interview.
